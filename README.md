# NCFO Ferry Capacity and Utilization  
**Draft Analytical Framework**

## Project Overview
The National Census of Ferry Operators (NCFO) is the only federally maintained, biennial census of ferry operations in the United States. While NCFO provides national coverage of ferry operators, vessels, terminals, and service activity, it has historically been used primarily for data maintenance and funding support. This project develops a system-level, descriptive analytical framework to assess ferry service capacity and utilization using NCFO data, supporting BTS’s mission as a decision-support agency and aligning with prior transportation research on ferry systems [1–4].

## Objectives
- Conduct a national, system-level descriptive assessment of ferry service capacity and utilization.
- Compare utilization across operators, route segments, and geographic contexts.
- Examine relationships between utilization and vessel capacity, terminal access, and governance structure.
- Establish a simulation-ready framework for scenario-based interpretation of NCFO data.

## Data Scope
- **Data source:** [National Census of Ferry Operators (NCFO)](https://www.bts.gov/NCFO)
- **Key elements:** operators, route segments, terminals, vessels, trips, passenger and vehicle boardings.
- **Scope:** national coverage; fixed-route ferry services; descriptive and comparative analysis.

### NCFO Documentation
- **Methodology:** [NCFO Survey Methodology](https://www.bts.gov/browse-statistical-products-and-data/surveys/national-census-ferry-operators-ncfo/Methodology)

## Analytical and Simulation Framework (High-Level)
- Organize NCFO data into a multi-layer system framework linking operators, assets, network structure, and service activity.
- Use descriptive analysis to identify patterns and variability in capacity and utilization.
- Develop lightweight simulation scenarios combining:
  - vessel-based capacity envelopes,
  - observed demand where available,
  - distribution-based demand scenarios where data are incomplete.
- Apply data-driven grouping to support transparent scenario assumptions and sensitivity analysis.
- Emphasize interpretability, robustness, and scenario exploration rather than prediction or optimization.

## Value to BTS
- Demonstrates practical, policy-facing use of NCFO data.
- Supports consistent interpretation of ferry capacity and utilization metrics.
- Provides a national synthesis that complements existing case-study-based ferry research.
- Establishes a foundation for future analytical and simulation work aligned with TRB interests [1–4].
